We are a highly cohesive team, meeting multiple times each week in joint sub-team meetings, full team meetings, and other groupings. This close engagement and constant collaboration between groups allows us to identify the accomplishments and goals of each subteam.

Drive Systems

The Drive Systems subteam is responsible for the design of the chassis, electronics housing, and locomotion of the rover. On the surface of Mars, there are many obstacles in the terrain that rovers must overcome to complete their missions. In competition, our rover must perform in similar geological conditions. Therefore, it is the responsibility of Drive Systems to design a locomotion system that:

  • Navigates over uneven terrain
  • Travels over extremely dusty and fine sediment
  • Performs precision turning and steering
  • Minimizes power consumption
  • Drives at moderate speed in order to complete entirety of tasks under competition specified time limit

Task Systems

The Task Systems subteam is responsible for overseeing the design, analysis, manufacturing, and testing of all aspects of the rover necessary for completing the competition tasks. This includes the robotic arm, storage units, end attachments, and any other components geared toward competition. Anything not to do with driving and controlling the rover is accomplished by the Task Systems subteam, therefore making it vital for the success of the rover at competition. Tasks range from collecting soil samples to carrying packages and surveying the land. The Task Systems has a wide range of things to accomplish and therefore the design of the arm and other parts must be thoroughly planned and implemented.

Controls Software

The Controls Software subteam is responsible for developing software to communicate with the rover's hardware. Using ROS (the Robot Operating System), the team interprets input data coming from remote controls and makes all necessary components of the rover behave accordingly. This team also makes decisions on additional features such as GPS and communications components so that the rover may talk and be controlled by a remote station.

Controls Electrical

While the other subteams focus on building the chassis, the Controls Electrical subteam builds the E-Core which houses electrical components and wiring that are crucial for a functioning rover. Additionally, the team works on integrating, cameras, sensors, along with the battery power supply on board the rover.

Science

The Science subteam is responsible for the science behind the Sample Return Task, in which the rover must select and return a potentially interesting biological sample from the field. The Science team researches cyanobacteria, which are the primary colonizers in harsh conditions such as the Utah desert, and how they might be detected both by the rover and in the lab. The team employs a combination of visible and infrared spectroscopy, pH and electrical conductivity tests, and nitrogen-detecting techniques to measure cyanobacterial signals both in rock samples and in culture.

Business

The Business subteam is responsible for managing the administrative, financial, logistic, and PR/marketing functions of the project team. This sub-team handles everything from coordinating sponsors to organizing team social events, ordering team apparel, and running a positive community outreach program. While the mechanical and controls subteams take care of building the rover itself, the Business subteam ensures all of the non-technical operations run smoothly, both behind the scenes and in the public eye.
